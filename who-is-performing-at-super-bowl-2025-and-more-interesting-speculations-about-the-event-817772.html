Who Is Performing at Super Bowl 2025, and More Interesting Speculations About the Event

September 2024 · 10 minute read

The Super Bowl is a massive event in America, bringing together hundreds of millions to watch the highly anticipated game. Equally significant is the halftime show, a coveted spectacle. In 2024, Usher headlined the display and made a truly unforgettable event out of it. Though people are still discussing the show of this year, there are some predictions already being made for the next!

Although the official announcement usually comes in September, speculation about the event is already rampant. It’s still too early to know for sure who will perform, but the anticipation is building. We decided to take a sneak peek at the speculations for who is performing at Super Bowl 2025!

Performances of Super Bowl 2023 and 2024 took the world by the storm.

TIMOTHY A. CLARY/AFP/East News

The Apple Music Super Bowl LVII Halftime Show, also referred to as the Super Bowl LVII halftime performance, captivated audiences during the February 12, 2023 event at State Farm Stadium in Glendale, Arizona. The spotlight was on Barbadian sensation Rihanna as she headlined the show, delivering a memorable performance. The performance was so loved by the public, that we still talk about it even these days!

TIMOTHY A. CLARY/AFP/East News

After Usher’s memorable performance at the last Super Bowl halftime show, everyone is curious about who will headline next. Over the past decade, stars like Beyoncé, Rihanna, Jennifer Lopez, Shakira, The Weeknd, and Bruno Mars have wowed audiences with their incredible performances. And it is not surprising when stars want to participate in such a grand show like Super Bowl. Besides being a part of a memorable event, participation in the Super Bowl is a significant marketing and popularity boost!

  • Spotify reported Jennifer Lopez’s music got a lot more popular after the 2020 Super Bowl, jumping by 335 percent.
  • Shakira’s streams also went up by 230 percent. According to Billboard, the songs they sang sold 16,000 digital copies that day, way more than the roughly 1,000 sold the day before
  • Billboard also mentioned that Lady Gaga’s sales shot up by 1,000 percent after her 2017 performance.
  • Forbes shared that Dr. Dre’s album sales rose by 183 percent after the 2022 Super Bowl.
  • Mary J Blige’s albums jumped by 999 percent. Variety reported Rihanna’s Spotify streams soared by over 640 percent across the U.S., and her song “B***h Better Have My Money” spiked by more than 2,600 percent.

For the 2025 Super Bowl halftime show, discussions are already buzzing with potential performers. While no official announcement has been made yet, rumors are circulating about who might take the stage. The excitement for who is performing at Super Bowl 2025, with fans, experts, and even bookmakers speculating about potential headliners.

Prediction one: Lil Wayne

Evan Agostini/Invision/East News

Lil Wayne, also known as Dwayne Michael Carter Jr., is recognized as a Packers fan. With the game set to be played at the home of the Saints, this could lead the NFL to consider other options.

Lil Wayne’s most recent mixtape, Tha Fix Before the VI, dropped in September. Fans are eagerly anticipating the release of two albums scheduled for 2024: Tha Carter VI and I Am Not a Human Being III. The timing of these releases could influence Lil Wayne’s eligibility for the halftime show. If one album is delayed until early 2025, it might signal his potential participation.

Currently, the odds are suggesting a 22.73% chance of him performing, according to the people’s betting. His music style and past legal issues might not align with the broad audience of the Super Bowl.

While Lil Wayne’s fandom and upcoming music releases could make him a contender, other factors, such as his legal history and the suitability of his music for the event, must be considered. As the Super Bowl approaches, it’ll be interesting to see who ultimately takes the stage for the halftime show.

Prediction two: Miley Cyrus

VALERIE MACON/AFP/East News

Miley Cyrus is reportedly in the mix for the 2025 Super Bowl halftime show, according to Sportsbookreview. However, no solid confirmation has surfaced yet, despite widespread tweets suggesting otherwise.

Last year, there was much talk about Miley headlining the halftime show, but those speculations faded when Usher was announced in September 2023. However, fresh off winning two Grammys and delivering a stunning performance of Flowers, Miley’s name is back in the spotlight. Fans are eager for her to take the stage.

2024 is shaping up to be a significant year for Miley. Her mother, Tish, hinted that she’s working on her next album. Despite Miley’s past aversion to touring and performing for large crowds, could she make an exception for the Super Bowl halftime show? Fans are hopeful, especially given Miley’s track record of delivering electrifying performances. Only time will tell if she’ll grace the Super Bowl stage in 2025, but the excitement and speculation continue to grow.

Prediction three: Taylor Swift

Casey Flanigan/imageSPACE/Associated Press/East News

No conversation about the Super Bowl halftime show would be complete without mentioning Taylor Swift. Taylor gained attention during the 2023/24 NFL season due to her relationship with Travis Kelce of the Kansas City Chiefs. Many believe she could make an even bigger impact by headlining her own halftime show.

Sportsbookreview lists Taylor as one of the early favorites for the 2025 show. Despite reportedly declining the opportunity in the past, citing a “long-term partnership” with Coca-Cola, 2025 could be the right time for Taylor to shine. By early 2025, she will have completed her record-breaking Eras tour and might have regained ownership of all her music through re-recordings.

Finishing off a successful few years with a Super Bowl halftime show where she performs songs she now fully owns? It’s bound to draw massive ratings! Fans are eagerly anticipating the possibility of seeing Taylor Swift take the stage and deliver a performance to remember.

Miley’s success at the prestigious ceremony, where she triumphed over Taylor Swift, hints at her potential to secure the coveted Super Bowl gig. Taylor, who reportedly declined the opportunity to headline the last two Super Bowls due to prior commitments, may not be available for next year’s event. Her concerns may include missing out if Travis Kelce and the Chiefs reach the 2025 Super Bowl.

With Taylor’s announcement of a new album, The Tortured Poets Department, scheduled for release on April 19, 2024, she may be preoccupied with planning a tour for the record early next year. Despite this, Taylor still holds odds of +200 to headline the halftime show, showcasing her immense star power.

Taylor’s relationship with Kelce has reportedly elevated the NFL and the Chiefs’ brand, particularly among young women, leading to increased sales of Kelce’s replica jerseys. Should Taylor headline a halftime show in the near future, it’s expected to further enhance the NFL’s popularity.

Prediction four: Drake

When discussing potential Super Bowl headliners, Drake, a frequent collaborator and former mentee of Lil Wayne, often enters the conversation. He’s also listed on Sportsbookreview’s odds lists, but there hasn’t been any indication or confirmation that he’s been approached to headline.

Drake would undoubtedly be a strong choice, given his immense popularity as one of the biggest artists globally. However, whether he’ll headline in 2025 is uncertain. If Lil Wayne were to headline, there’s a possibility that Drake could join him on stage as a guest performer. Drake’s massive fan base and chart-topping hits make him a compelling candidate for the halftime show. His dynamic stage presence and ability to engage audiences could make for a memorable performance.

While rumors and speculations continue to swirl, only time will tell who will ultimately take the spotlight at the 2025 Super Bowl halftime show. Fans eagerly await any announcements or developments regarding the lineup for the highly anticipated event.

Prediction 5: Billie Eilish

Billie Eilish made a big splash at the recent Grammy Awards, clinching the Song of the Year award for What Was I Made For? This win not only marked a triumph for Billie but also prevented Taylor from securing her first Song of the Year Grammy. Now, the question arises: could Billie also steal the spotlight from Taylor by headlining the Super Bowl halftime show next year?

According to the best betting apps, Billie stands as one of the favorites, to take on the halftime performance. Following closely behind is London-born artist Dua Lipa. Both artists have the talent to shine on one of America’s grandest stages. Similarly, Brits Ed Sheeran and Adele could also captivate audiences with their performances. The speculation surrounding potential headliners for the Super Bowl halftime show continues to grow, with fans eagerly awaiting any official announcements. Whoever ultimately secures the coveted spot is sure to deliver a performance to remember on one of the biggest stages in the world.
