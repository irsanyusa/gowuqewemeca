Cook Finds Sandwich Islands

1778-01-18 Captain James Cook stumbles over the Sandwich Islands (Hawaiian Islands)

  • 1778-11-26 British explorer Captain James Cook is the first European to visit Maui in the Sandwich Islands (now Hawaii)
  • 1792-03-04 Oranges introduced to Hawaii
  • 1803-08-09 First horses arrive in Hawaii
  • 1813-01-21 First reference to pineapple cultivation in Hawaii in diary entry by Francisco de Paula Marin
  • 1813-01-21 Spaniard Don Francisco de Paula y Marin plants coffee in Hawaii - 1st record of coffee growing in Hawaii
  • 1819-09-17 First whaling ship arrives in Hawaii
  • 1820-08-07 1st potatoes planted in Hawaii
  • 1822-01-07 1st printing in Hawaii of a ceremonial broadside for King Kamehameha II by Elisha Loomis
  • 1824-09-26 Kapiolani defies Pele (Hawaiian volcano goddess) & lives
  • 1825-04-10 1st hotel in Hawaii opens
  • 1835-05-13 1st foreign embassy in Hawaii forms
  • 1835-07-26 1st sugar cane plantation started in Hawaii
  • 1835-07-29 1st sugar plantation in Hawaii established
  • 1836-07-30 First English language newspaper published in Hawaii
  • 1839-06-07 Hawaiian Declaration of Rights is signed
  • 1839-06-17 King of Hawaii Kamehameha III issues Edict of toleration which gives Roman Catholics freedom to worship in the Hawaiian Islands. The Hawaii Catholic Church and the Cathedral of Our Lady of Peace is later established
  • 1840-10-08 "Ke Kumukānāwai a me nā Kānāwai o ko Hawaiʻi Pae ʻĀina, Honolulu, 1840", the 1st written Constitution of the Hawaiian Kingdom enacted by King Kamehameha III and Kuhina Nui (Prime Minister) Kekāuluohi
  • 1840-10-08 First Hawaiian constitution proclaimed
  • 1842-12-19 US recognizes independence of Hawaii
  • 1843-11-28 Ka Lahui: Hawaiian Independence Day - The Kingdom of Hawaii is officially recognized by the United Kingdom and France as an independent nation.
  • 1845-05-20 1st legislative assembly convenes in Hawaii
  • 1846-05-21 1st steamship arrives in Hawaii
  • 1847-03-10 1st money minted in Hawaii
  • 1847-09-10 1st theater opens in Hawaii
  • 1848-03-07 In Hawaii, Great Mahele (division of lands) signed
  • 1850-08-30 Honolulu, Hawaii, becomes a city
  • 1850-11-06 1st Hawaiian fire engine
  • 1850-12-20 Hawaiian post office established
  • 1850-12-27 Hawaiian Fire Department established
  • 1851-10-01 1st Hawaiian stamps issued
  • 1852-01-03 1st Chinese arrive in Hawaii
  • 1852-12-17 1st Hawaiian cavalry organized
  • 1853-10-19 First flour mill in Hawaii begins operations
  • 1857-09-30 US occupies Sand, Baker, Howland & Jarvis Island, south of Hawaii
  • 1858-08-17 1st bank in Hawaii opens
  • 1859-01-23 Mauna Loa volcano in Hawaii begins an eruption that lasts 300 days
  • 1859-09-02 Gas lighting introduced to Hawaii
  • 1860-08-08 Queen of Sandwich Islands (Hawaii) arrives in NYC
  • 1865-02-04 Hawaiian Board of Education formed
  • 1868-04-03 A Hawaiian surfs on highest wave ever - a 50-foot tidal wave
  • 1869-04-30 Hawaiian YMCA organized
  • 1870-12-19 After 31 days at sea in a small boat, William Halford and 3 others reach the island of Kauai, Hawaii to seek help for the shipwrecked USS Saginaw. A capsize in the breakers meant only Halford survived.
  • 1874-03-18 Hawaii signs a treaty with the United States granting exclusive trading rights.

King Kalakaua's US Visit

1874-12-12 Hawaiian King David Kalakaua is 1st king to visit the US as guest of Ulysses S. Grant at 1st US state dinner at the White House

  • 1875-05-31 Reciprocity Treaty between US & Hawaii ratified
  • 1877-07-23 First telephone and telegraph line in Hawaii is completed
  • 1878-07-20 1st telephone introduced in Hawaii
  • 1878-09-30 1st Portuguese immigrants arrive in Hawaii
  • 1879-03-01 Library of Hawaii founded
  • 1879-07-17 1st railroad opens in Hawaii

  • 1885-02-09 1st Japanese arrive in Hawaii
  • 1887-11-29 US receives rights to Pearl Harbor, on Oahu, Hawaii
  • 1889-11-18 Oahu Railway begins public service in Hawaii
  • 1889-12-19 Bishop Museum founded in Hawaii
  • 1891-01-29 Liliuokalani is proclaimed Queen of Hawaii, its last monarch
  • 1892-01-11 Hawaiian Historical Society is founded
  • 1892-06-18 Brothers Edward and Robert Jordan plant 1st Macadamia nuts in Oahu, Hawaii
  • 1893-01-17 Queen Liliuokalani is deposed by an American coup, Kingdom of Hawaii becomes a republic
  • 1894-07-04 Republic of Hawaii proclaimed, Sanford B Dole as president
  • 1894-10-29 First election of Hawaiian Republic
  • 1895-11-26 Hawaiian Sugar Planters Association forms
  • 1896-10-27 1st Pali Road completed in Hawaii (winds so strong streams flow UP!)
  • 1897-02-05 1st showing of a motion picture in Hawaii at the Hawaiian Opera House
  • 1897-06-16 A treaty annexing the Republic of Hawaii to the United States is signed; the Republic would not be dissolved until a year later
  • 1898-06-15 US House of Representatives passes resolution to annex Hawaii
  • 1898-07-06 US Senate agrees to annex Hawaii
  • 1898-07-07 US President McKinley signs the Organic Act to annex Hawaii
  • 1898-08-12 Hawaii is formally annexed to US
  • 1899-12-12 1st case of plague on Oahu, Hawaii
  • 1900-04-30 The "Hawaiian Organic Act" is enacted by US Congress making Hawaii a US territory
  • 1900-06-14 Having been annexed to the USA on 12 August 1898, Hawaii is constituted as an organized territory
  • 1901-02-20 1st territorial legislature of Hawaii convenes
  • 1901-03-02 Hawaii's first telegraph company opens
  • 1902-12-28 Trans-Pacific cable links Hawaii to US
  • 1903-01-05 SF-Hawaii telegraph cable opens for public use
  • 1906-04-26 Motion pictures begin regular showings at the Orpheum Theater in Honolulu, Hawaii
  • 1907-04-30 Honolulu, Hawaii, becomes an independent city.
  • 1909-11-11 Construction of US navy base begins at Pearl Harbor, Hawaii
  • 1915-03-25 1st submarine disaster; a US F-4 sinks off Hawaii, killing 21
  • 1916-08-01 Hawaii National Park forms
  • 1917-06-21 Hawaiian Red Cross forms
  • 1918-06-28 1st flight between Hawaiian Islands
  • 1922-02-09 Snow on Mauna Loa, Hawaii
  • 1924-09-09 Hanapepe Massacre occurs on Kauai, Hawaii
  • 1927-06-29 1st flight from West Coast arrives in Hawaii
  • 1927-07-14 1st commercial airplane flight in Hawaii
  • 1928-05-01 Lei Day begins (a Hawaiian celebration)
  • 1931-04-27 100°F (38°C), Pahala, Hawaii (state record)
  • 1935-07-05 1st "Hawaii Calls" radio program is broadcast
  • 1936-10-22 1st commercial flight from mainland to Hawaii

Roman Catholic Diocese of Honolulu

1941-01-25 Pope Pius XII elevates the Apostolic Vicariate of the Hawaiian Islands to the dignity of a diocese. It becomes the Roman Catholic Diocese of Honolulu.

  • 1941-12-07 Imperial Japanese Navy attacks US Naval Air Station Kaneohe Bay, Hawaii; 18 sailors killed, 33 of 36 patrol planes destroyed or damaged, 1st Japanese aircraft shot down in action
  • 1944-05-21 WWII: West Loch Disaster - explosion during munition loading kills at least 160 sailors, injures nearly 400, destroys six ships and damages 3 piers and several buildings at Pearl Harbor U.S. Naval Base in Oahu, Hawaii; details were kept classified until the early 1960s
  • 1946-04-01 Tsunamis generated by a quake in Aleutian Trench strike Hilo, Hawaii
