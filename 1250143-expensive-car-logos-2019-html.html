There are several expensive car logos which have stood out in the automobile world. These unique emblems ensure that the brands remain relevant and retain their loyal customers.

All vehicles have a distinctive car emblem to enable their potential buyers to have an easy time locating a particular brand. Some car logos have animals like a horse, lion, bull, jaguar and ram while others have symbols that portray different meanings.

Expensive car logos and names list

Below are some of the most common car logos we thought might interest you.

Ferrari

The first Ferrari was created in the early 1950s. Manufactured by Enzo, Ferrari has committedly produced one of the best luxury racing cars in the world. With improved engineering, Ferrari has revolutionized the luxury car industry.

It was later when Enzo Ferrari met a heroic airman’s(Francesco Baracca) parents and was convinced by the mother to include the prancing horse to his cars to bring good luck. The logo was later advanced by adding canary yellow to the background but maintains the original black prancing horse.

Lamborghini

The owner, Ferruccio Lamborghini, founded the business by making tractors from reconfigured surplus military machines. Ferruccio's extreme interest in Spanish bullfighting is displayed in the logo. The golden bull represents consistency, power and fortitude while the black colour denotes prestige, elegance and integrity.

Porsche

Porsche was founded by Ferdinand Porsche who was the chief engineer at Mercedes Benz. The first Porsche was designed and launched in 1938, gaining popularity and propelling Ferdinand to success until present times.

The Porsche logo was based on the Coat of Arms of the Free People State.

Toyota

Started as "Toyoda" and later changed to Toyota due to three main reasons:

  • It depicted the company's growth from a family business to a broader-based social entity.
  • The name Toyota sounded more pleasant to the ear.
  • Its eight-stroke count in the Japanese language was associated with wealth and good fortune.

    • The new Toyota mark and logo were later introduced on October 2, 1989, and have been in use ever since.
